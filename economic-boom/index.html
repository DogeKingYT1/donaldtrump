Trump's Economic Boom

The Trump administration ushered in a period of unprecedented economic growth, characterized by deregulation, tax cuts, and a focus on American workers. This section explores the key policies and their impact on the American economy.

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) of 2017 was a landmark piece of legislation that significantly reformed the U.S. tax code. Key provisions included a permanent reduction in the corporate tax rate from 35% to 21%, a 20% deduction for pass-through entities, and increased standard deductions for individuals.

GDP Growth

The U.S. economy experienced robust GDP growth during the Trump administration, with an average annual growth rate of 2.3%. The growth rates were 2.99% in 2017, 2.13% in 2018, and 3.35% in 2019, before the COVID-19 pandemic.

Record-Low Unemployment

Unemployment rates for several minority groups reached historic lows during the Trump administration. The Black unemployment rate reached a historic low of 5.3% in 2019, the Hispanic unemployment rate reached a record low of 3.9% in 2019, and the Asian American unemployment rate fell to 2.1% in 2019, its lowest level on record.

Wage Growth

Real wages for American workers saw significant growth during the Trump administration. From January 2017 to January 2020, individual real wage growth was 1.4% per annum. By the end of President Trump's term, real wages had increased by 7.1%.
