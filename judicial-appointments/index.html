Trump's Judicial Appointments: A Lasting Legacy

During his four years in office, President Donald Trump appointed a total of 279 federal judges, including three Supreme Court justices. This represents a historic transformation of the federal judiciary, with a focus on appointing judges who are committed to a textualist and originalist interpretation of the Constitution. This section explores the significance of these appointments and their lasting impact on the nation.

Supreme Court Appointments

Neil Gorsuch

Justice Gorsuch is a proponent of textualism and originalism. His nomination was a fulfillment of President Trump's promise to appoint a successor to Justice Antonin Scalia who shared his judicial philosophy.

Brett Kavanaugh

Justice Kavanaugh's nomination was highly contentious, but his confirmation solidified a conservative majority on the Supreme Court. He is known for his textualist approach to statutory interpretation.

Amy Coney Barrett

Justice Barrett's appointment further shifted the balance of the Supreme Court. Like her fellow Trump appointees, she is a committed originalist and textualist.
